❓ Help & First-Time Setup

Follow these steps in order. Most of it is one-time setup. You can copy the values straight into Settings.

0 · Secure your account

  • Default login is admin / admin123. Set a strong password before going live — use the ADMIN_PASSWORD env var on first run, or change it later via the change-password API.
  • This panel has one job: manage WhatsApp chats, contacts, broadcasts and leads. Everything is stored on your own server — no third-party SaaS.

1 · Create a Meta (Facebook) app

  1. Go to developers.facebook.com/apps and click Create App.
  2. Choose Business as the type. Give it any name.
  3. In the app dashboard, add the WhatsApp product (for chat) and keep Facebook Login available (for Lead Ads).
  4. Note your App ID and App Secret (under App Settings → Basic). You'll paste these in Settings.

2 · Connect WhatsApp (Cloud API)

  1. In your Meta app → WhatsApp → API Setup.
  2. Copy the Phone Number ID (not the phone number itself) → paste in Settings.
  3. Generate a permanent Access Token: create a System User in Business Settings, give it the WhatsApp permissions, and generate a token that never expires. Paste it in Settings as the Access Token. (The temporary 24-hour token also works for testing.)
  4. Pick any secret word as your Verify Token (e.g. myverify123) and paste the same value in Settings.
  5. In WhatsApp → Configuration → Webhook, set:
    • Callback URL:
    • Verify Token: the same word from step 4
    Then click Verify and Save, and under webhook fields Subscribe to messages.
  6. Save Settings. The Dashboard should now show ● Connected.

The webhook needs a public HTTPS URL. On localhost, use a tunnel like ngrok and use that https address as the callback URL. On a live server, use your domain.

3 · Turn on the AI assistant (optional, free)

  1. Get a free API key from Google AI Studio (Gemini) or Groq. Paste it in Settings → AI assistant.
  2. In the Business info / FAQ box, paste everything the AI should know: what you sell, prices, timings, policies, common questions. The AI answers only from this.
  3. Keep AI enabled and AI auto-reply ON checked. Now every WhatsApp message gets an instant reply, even at night.
  4. In any chat, tick AI paused to take over manually for that one person.
  5. No key? It still works with a built-in basic auto-reply — just less smart.

4 · Connect Meta Lead Ads (auto-import leads)

  1. Make sure App ID and App Secret are saved in Settings (from step 1).
  2. In your Meta app → Facebook Login → Settings, add this as a Valid OAuth Redirect URI:
  3. Go to WhatsApp → Contacts tab here and click Connect Facebook. Approve your Pages.
  4. Done. New lead-form submissions auto-import as contacts (tagged by campaign), and the app also re-checks every 5 minutes.
  5. To pull old leads once, open in the browser while logged in.

5 · Sending messages

  • Replies in a chat are free within 24 hours of the customer's last message. The green/amber banner shows the window.
  • Broadcasts & sequences need an approved template. Create templates in Meta Business Manager → they appear in the Templates tab once approved.
  • Broadcast: pick an audience (paste numbers / all / by tag), enter the template name, send. Optional schedule for later.
  • Sequences: set up a drip of template messages auto-sent over days; auto-enroll anyone with a chosen tag.
  • Marketing template messages are billed by Meta on your own account (~₹0.8 each in India). Set your rate in Settings to track spend.

6 · Capturing website leads (optional)

Point any website contact form at this endpoint to collect leads here too:

POST 
Content-Type: application/json

{ "source": "website", "name": "...", "phone": "...", "email": "...", "message": "..." }

7 · Daily use

  • WhatsApp → Chats: live inbox. A sound + badge alerts you to new messages from any tab.
  • WhatsApp → Contacts: your audience, with tags. Import/export CSV.
  • Leads: every enquiry (website, Meta, WhatsApp) in one list. Export to CSV anytime.
  • Analytics: delivery/read rates and per-campaign performance.
